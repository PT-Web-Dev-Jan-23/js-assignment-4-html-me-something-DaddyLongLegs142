My favorite passages from Douglas Adams' Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy

Vogon Poetry

¶ According to Adams, Vogon poetry is regarded as the third worst in the universe. 

                  "Oh freddled gruntbuggly,
                  thy micturations are to me
                  as plurdled gaggleblotchits
                  on a lurgid bee
                  groop I implore thee
                  my foonting turlingdromes
                  and hooptiously drangle me
                  with crinkly bindlewurdles
                  or I will rend thee in the gabberworts
                  with my blurglecruncheon
                  see if I don't!"

Government

¶ Adams also had a unique perspective on governance.

“The major problem—one of the major problems, for there are several—one of the many major problems with governing people is that of whom you get to do it; or rather of who manages to get people to let them do it to them. To summarize: it is a well-known fact that those people who must want to rule people are, ipso facto, those least suited to do it. To summarize the summary: anyone who is capable of getting themselves made President should on no account be allowed to do the job.”

Design

¶ And he had a valuable insight when it comes to how we design things.

“A common mistake that people make when trying to design something completely foolproof is to underestimate the ingenuity of complete fools.”
All quotes come from Douglas Adams' Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy series.
